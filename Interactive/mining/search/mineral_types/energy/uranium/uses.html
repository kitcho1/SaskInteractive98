Uses of Uranium and Radiation

Use Overview More Detail
Electricity Electricity is used to heat our homes, operate appliances, light our dwellings and the list goes on. The manufacture of this product requires energy that must come from some source. Uranium is one of many sources used to generate electricity.
Medicine An x-ray at the doctor's is one of the most common ways of using radiation in the medical field. Radiation is used more and more frequently in medicine as a safe, diagnostic tool
Industry Medicine is not the only field to use radiation as a diagnostic tool; industry relies on radioisotopes to detect welding problems or in the manufacture of steel products. Even the smoke detector in your home uses radioisotopes.
Agriculture Radiation is used to reduce the spoilage of certain foods.
Nuclear Weaponry Nuclear weapons require a source of energy and uranium, plutonium and radioactive hydrogen provide this source. No nuclear weapons are made using Saskatchewan uranium.
Uranium Metal An extremely dense metal, left-over uranium has many uses, including providing a shield to radiation.
