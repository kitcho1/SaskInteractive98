Radiation has been used for over 30 years in the agriculture and food industry. Food spoilage can be reduced by treating agricultural products with ionizing radiation. The harmful microorganisms that would normally contribute to spoilage are destroyed. In addition, radiation

  • is used to slow ripening of fruits and vegetables.
  • can help eliminate bacteria that cause botulism.
  • can control insects.

How is this done?

Gamma rays or x-rays from cobalt 60 or cesium 137 are passed through food such as dried spices, potatoes, wheat, pork, or fresh fruits and vegetables. Food irradiation extends the shelf life of the food and causes no flavour or texture changes. It can reduce the necessity of using pesticides and the incidence of botulism and salmonella. The food does not become radioactive and benzene is produced. In Canada, irradiation is listed on the food label as an additive.
